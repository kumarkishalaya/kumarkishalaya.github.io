Nov 10, 2024

I recently saw a documentary where our evolutionary closest cousins - chimps were able to solve very short term memory tests way faster than humans. A reason behind this was cognitive trade off hypothesis which is pretty interesting. Some of the physically weaker chimps who lived on tree tops were driven out by their stronger counterparts down in to the forest and in to the dangerous grasslands of savannah. These chimps had to learn to stand up straight, develop language and pictures to educate their companions as well their future generations. They had to learn to remember past threats, predict and imagine future opportunities and share knowledge to be able to survive. In the process they lost their ability to hold very short term memories but they learnt how to imagine, share and build communities far more sophesticated than their evolutionary stronger cousins. Food for thought!
